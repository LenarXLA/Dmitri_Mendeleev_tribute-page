Dmitri Mendeleev

Everything You Need to Know About the Inventor of Periodic Table

Here's a time line of Dmitri Mendeleev's life:

  • 1834 - Born in in Tobolsk, Russia.
  • 1841-1849 - Primary education at the Tobolsk gymnasium.
  • 1850-1855 - Student at the Main Pedagogical Institute, St. Petersburg. Graduated with diploma of Senior Teacher.
  • 1855-1856 - Teacher at middle school in Odessa.
  • 1856 - Magister of Chemistry at the St.-Petersburg University.
  • 1857-1859 - Private Docent at the St.-Petersburg University.
  • 1859-1861 - Postgraduate researcher in Heidelberg, Germany.
  • 1861-1863 - Lecturer at several high schools in St.-Petersburg; handbook writing; editorial activity; technical consultancy for the Ministry of Finances.
  • 1864-1866 - Professor at the Technological Institute.
  • 1864 - Docent at the St.-Petersburg University.
  • 1865 - PhD in Chemistry.
  • 1865-1890 - Professor at the St.-Petersburg University.
  • 1865 - Prof. of Technical Chemistry.
  • 1867 - Prof. of Pure Chemistry.
  • 1869 - Formulating the periodic table of chemical elements.
  • 1885 - Emeritus Professor and lecturer.
  • 1891-1895 - Scientific consultant to the Navy Ministry.
  • 1892-1907 - Service at the Depot (Chamber) of Weights and Measures.
  • 1892 - Scientific keeper (since 1893: the Head of the Chamber).
  • 1907 - Mendeleev died at the age of 72 in Saint Petersburg from influenza.

"Dmitri Mendeleev is often referred to as the Father of the Periodic Table. He called his table or matrix, "the Periodic System"."

-- wikipedia.org
